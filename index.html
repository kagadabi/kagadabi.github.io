About Me

I have always been interested in the creative element of games and stories. Ever since I was a child, I started experimenting with engines such as Game Maker and RPG Maker XP as a creative outlet. In 2013, I got introduced to tabletop RPGs, such as D&D, Vampire: The Masquerade, Kids on Bikes, and Call of Cthulhu, and I have been playing and GMing since then. I mostly did it as a hobby, until 2020, when I started doing it professionally, as a freelance Dungeon Master. Over these years, I have accumulated hundreds of original characters, stories, and concepts.

In 2022, I started working formally as a Game Designer in the gaming industry, where I gained plenty of experience about game development, the use of bigger engines and tools, as well as team-based workflow and communication. I also developed an understanding of how to align design with technical constraints while collaborating across disciplines.

Working on games has given me some insight as to why I enjoy this process so much. The best part about the games I choose to make is that they always take an entirely new form when there is someone else experiencing them; it is feeling the synergy between my design and the player’s own perspective. Because of this passion for dynamic and evolving player experiences, I always strive to design systems and mechanics that empower players to feel creative and engaged.
